MediHome

Why MediHome?

Do personalized alerts regarding critical, patient-inputted data and colour coding data reduce the time physicians spend analyzing data, while simultaneously reducing the risk of error?

What is MediHome

MediHome collects data of patients and transmits it directly to their physician so they do not have to commute, and wait in a waiting room full of other sick people only to have their vitals measured.

Goal of this study

The goal of this study is to determine whether or not colour coding medical data would enable physicians to analyze data quicker and make less errors.

Your role

Your role is to analyze data on two different dashboards. YOu will have to tell us which patients we need to contact depending on the data they sent their physician that morning.

I am not a physician, how will I be able to interpret data?

In order to make it easier, we will not use actual medical metrics. Instead, we created the following scale:
Values between 1-5 = Good
Values between 6-10 = Concerning, but not life-threatening
Values between 11 and 15 = We need to contact the patient

How to do the survey

  • We will ask you to time yourself while you analyze each dashboard. Please write down all of the names of the patients who need to be contacted and for which vital sign they need to be contacted.
  • Restart the timer and do the same for the second dashboard. The second dashboard will be colour coded.
    (The names and the data will be shuffled, so please do not rely on memory.)
  • Then, fill out the survey and submit.
