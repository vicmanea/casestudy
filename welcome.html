MediHome

Why MediHome?

Do personalized alerts regarding critical, patient-inputted data and colour coding data reduce the time physicians spend analyzing data, while simultaneously reducing the risk of error?

What is MediHome


MediHome collects data of patients and transmits it directly to their physician so they do not have to commute, and wait in a waiting room full of other sick people only to have their vitals measured.


Goal of this study


The goal of this study is to determine whether or not colour coding medical data would enable physicians to analyze data quicker and make less errors.


Your role


Your role is to analyze data on two different dashboards. You will have to tell us which patients we need to contact depending on the data they sent their physician that morning. Patients who need to be contacted are those who have alarmingly low data inputted, or alarmingly high data. Please note that BMI does not need to be flagged, as it it not an accurate metric in determining someone's health.


I am not a physician, how will I be able to interpret data?


In order to make it easier, we created a table with the metrics and the classification of all ranges. This way, it is easier for non-medical professionals to analyze data.This table can be found in the navigation pane on the side.


How to do the survey


  • - We will ask you to time yourself while you analyze each dashboard. Please write down all of the names of the patients who need to be contacted and for which vital sign they need to be contacted.
    Patients with low/concerning or very high/alarming data need to be contacted.

  • - Restart the timer and do the same for the second dashboard. The second dashboard will be colour coded and can be found in the side navigation menu.
    (The names and the data will be shuffled, so please do not rely on memory.)

  • - Then, fill out the survey and submit.
